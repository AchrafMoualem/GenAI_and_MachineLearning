# 📊 Data Analysis in Action: The Case of Daesang

So I found this really interesting example of data analysis in action that I wanted to share with you.

You know how sometimes companies just guess what customers want? Well, there's this South Korean food company called **Daesang** that decided to stop guessing and actually use their data.

---

## The Problem They Had

Their sales people were doing everything manually. Like, imagine filling out reports by hand and tracking everything in Excel spreadsheets. This meant some sales people were doing great, others not so much, and they had no idea why. They couldn't spot trends until it was too late. And there was lots of wasted time on paperwork instead of actually selling.

---

## What They Did About It

They built this system that pulls together data from everywhere. Things like:

- What schools are ordering for lunch  
- Which menu items are trending at restaurants  
- Who's winning military supply contracts  
- How different clients buy over time  

And here's the really smart part. They didn't just hire data experts to do the analysis. They trained their actual sales people to work with the data. So now the folks who actually talk to customers can spot patterns themselves.

---

## The Results Are Pretty Wild

- They cut the time needed to plan sales strategies by **82 percent**.  
- They saved over **5,000 hours of work per year**, which is like **2.5 people working full time**.  
- They started winning contracts they would've missed before.  
- They can now customize proposals for each client based on real data, not just gut feelings.  

---

## What Would've Happened Without the Data?

Honestly, they'd still be guessing. Some sales person might have a gut feeling that a certain restaurant chain is ready to buy more, but without data they wouldn't know if that's actually true. They'd miss opportunities that competitors would grab.

---

## The Coolest Part

They're now moving into predictive stuff, trying to forecast what customers will want before the customers even know they want it. That's the dream, right?

---

## Why This Story Matters

What I like about this story is that it's not about replacing humans with computers. It's about giving regular people better tools to do their jobs. The sales folks still do the selling, they just don't have to waste time on spreadsheets anymore.
